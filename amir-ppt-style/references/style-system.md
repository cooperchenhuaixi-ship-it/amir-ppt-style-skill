# Amir PPT Style System

## Source Pattern

This system is distilled from five 8-slide decks: product launch, media kit, investor deck, proposal deck, and campaign deck. The common language is not a conventional template. It is closer to editorial art direction: large type, high-contrast page rhythm, minimal but precise content blocks, and one strong visual motif per deck.

## Core Principles

1. Make the headline the composition.
   - Use titles as the primary visual object, not just as labels.
   - Typical title sizes on 1920x1080: 72-180px/pt equivalent.
   - Allow two-line titles when they create drama; avoid accidental line breaks.

2. Use tiny navigational chrome.
   - Top-left section markers are small, calm, and consistent.
   - Examples: `01 · THE BRIEF`, `04 · CHANNEL MIX`, `PRODUCT LAUNCH · 2026`.
   - Keep markers around 14-18pt, often in accent color.

3. Build contrast into every slide.
   - Huge title versus tiny label.
   - Dark canvas versus neon accent.
   - Flat bright background versus small dark UI-like details.
   - Sparse cover/closing pages versus denser data pages.

4. Use one motif per deck.
   - Dark-tech: fluid neon ribbons, blue/violet glow, dot matrix, glass panels.
   - Bold-proposal: solid flat backgrounds, massive type, small tags, minimal cards.
   - Soft-media: pastel glow, editorial serif/sans pairing, creator photography, airy metrics.

5. Keep commercial storylines compact.
   - Default deck length: 8 slides.
   - Every slide gets one narrative job.
   - Write slide titles as claims or labels with momentum, not generic headings.

## Routes

### dark-tech

Use for AI, SaaS, investor, product launch, automation, fintech, and serious campaign strategy.

Palette:
- Base: `#0A0A0A`, `#14101F`, `#0B0B0B`
- Electric blue: `#4D5BFF`, `#549EFF`, `#4FA8FF`
- Muted data text: `#8FA3C8`
- White: `#FFFFFF`
- Panel fills: rgba blue/black/white at 8-55% opacity

Typography:
- Primary: Space Grotesk, Geist, Inter
- Section label: semi-bold, 14-18pt
- Title: medium/semi-bold, 76-180pt
- Body: 16-26pt depending on density
- Metrics: 90-180pt for hero numbers

Layout moves:
- Put the giant title left or top-left; keep generous margins around 90-190px.
- Use one large abstract gradient or ribbon as background texture.
- Add translucent panels only after the title hierarchy is clear.
- For data slides, use a row of quiet cards with one oversized metric.
- Use dot matrices or grid bars as texture, usually bottom-right or behind content.

Slide recipes:
- Cover: large promise line, tiny label, one button-like pill, abstract neon motif.
- Problem/objective: left title, right translucent metric block.
- Insight: oversized percentage or number with short explanation lines.
- Mechanism: three or four glass cards, each with a one-word stage.
- Economics/KPIs: black canvas, blue accent rows, small charts or bars.
- Closing: one large direct line and a single small CTA pill.

### bold-proposal

Use for proposals, agency decks, creative briefs, commercial plans, campaign pitches, and fast executive narratives.

Palette:
- Cream: `#F3ECDD`
- Yellow: `#FFE14D`
- Lime: `#C6F23C`
- Lavender: `#C9B8FF`
- Mint: `#BFE9CF`
- Orange: `#FF7A2F`
- Electric violet: `#5B4BFF`
- Black: `#0B0B0B`
- Ink tones: `#29241B`, `#3A2C00`, `#103627`, `#233D0C`, `#4E2308`, `#2B1C63`

Typography:
- Primary: DM Sans or another clean geometric sans.
- Titles can be enormous, often 150-180pt.
- Use thin, small captions and tags for contrast.

Layout moves:
- Change background color every slide to create rhythm.
- Use full-canvas color instead of cards or section containers.
- Let one huge word dominate: `Brief`, `Approach`, `Scope`, `Timeline`, `Team`, `Invest`.
- Add small pill tags, white labels, or thin lines as editorial detail.
- Keep content blocks low and horizontal when the title is huge.

Slide recipe:
1. Cream cover with title and tiny project tags.
2. Yellow brief page with one massive word and small floating labels.
3. Lime approach page with three steps along the bottom.
4. Lavender scope page with a 2x3 grid, but keep the title oversized.
5. Mint timeline page with a horizontal process line.
6. Orange team page with simple portraits or initials.
7. Violet investment/pricing page with three compact cards.
8. Black closing page with a huge action line.

### soft-media

Use for media kits, creator profiles, portfolios, talent decks, lifestyle products, and partnership proposals.

Palette:
- White and off-white canvases.
- Black editorial pages.
- Pastel glow: pink, magenta, cyan, lemon, lavender.
- Text: mostly `#0B0B0B`, `#000000`, `#FFFFFF`.
- Use translucent black or white overlays at 8-45%.

Typography:
- Pair Inter or similar sans with a stylish editorial serif such as Fraunces when available.
- Use title sizes around 116-158pt.
- Body stays small and elegant.

Layout moves:
- Use soft blurred glow fields at edges or corners.
- Combine large title type with small creator/photo cards.
- Metrics can sit in a clean row with tiny sparklines or lines.
- Portfolio pages can use dark canvas with image thumbnails and subtle magenta glow.

Slide recipe:
- Cover: name or direct creator identity, black or white canvas, edge glow.
- Bio: one portrait, short paragraph, oversized rotated or cropped word.
- Audience: large metric plus compact demographic chart.
- Reach: metrics in a row with small accent marks.
- Platforms: list with numbers aligned right.
- Work: thumbnail strip on black.
- Packages: three pale cards, one highlighted.
- Closing: large friendly line.

## Typography Scale

Use these as starting points on 16:9 widescreen slides:

- Section marker: 14-18pt.
- Tiny metadata: 10-14pt.
- Body: 16-24pt.
- Card heading: 20-30pt.
- Standard slide title: 72-120pt.
- Poster title: 140-180pt.
- Hero metric: 90-180pt.

If the deck tool uses pixels, treat these as approximate px values on a 1920x1080 canvas.

## Composition Checklist

- Does the slide have one dominant object?
- Is the title big enough to feel designed, not merely large?
- Is the section marker small and consistent?
- Is the visual motif repeated across at least three slides?
- Are cards translucent or minimal enough to stay secondary?
- Is body copy reduced to the minimum useful words?
- Does the slide rhythm alternate sparse and dense moments?
- Does the closing slide land as a direct action line?

## Avoid

- Generic title/subtitle PowerPoint layouts.
- Dense bullet lists.
- Decorative icon grids.
- Rounded-card dashboards as the primary style.
- Multiple unrelated gradients in the same deck.
- Reusing original source deck screenshots or proprietary assets without explicit permission.
- `Thank you` as the final slide when a stronger closing line is possible.
